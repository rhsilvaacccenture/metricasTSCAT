Risks & Recommendations

Critical gaps to address to sustain projected efficiency gains
⚠️ Key Risks
💰
Management Overrun
889.5h spent vs 40h estimated across R1–R3 — 2,124% over budget. Formalise a management allocation of 150–200h per release.
🔁
Technical Debt Recurrence
R1's 2,111h UAT burden shows how inherited debt can mask true performance. Implement technical debt tracking per release.
📋
R3 Estimation Gaps
5 USs still without estimates — final R3 figures could shift the baseline for simulation.
🚀
Deploy at 0h Actual
186h estimated but 0h spent across all releases. Review if deploy effort is captured elsewhere or estimates should be adjusted.
✅ Recommendations
📐
Re-baseline Estimation Methodology
The current x4 multiplication factor overestimates by 30–40% for mature releases. Adjust to reflect actual team velocity.
📅
Quarterly Capacity Review
Conduct quarterly reviews to validate simulation assumptions against actuals and recalibrate projections. Addressing risks now will protect the projected ~2,950h in cumulative R4–R6 savings.

Notes & Clarifications

Context for accurate interpretation of the metrics
📌
Data Extraction Date
Metrics extracted on Tuesday, April 14th. Subsequent ALMA reporting may show updated figures. This report reflects a snapshot subject to change in the next cycle.
✖️
Estimation Methodology – x4 Factor
All total estimations include a 4x multiplication factor applied to the base technical estimation. E.g. if a US is technically estimated at 10h, the total estimation used is 40h, accounting for full delivery effort across all areas.
⚠️
R1 – DT Hours & UAT R1
DT hours in R1 (3,183h) include 2,111h of UAT R1: (1) technical debt from previous UATs + UAT from December, and (2) R2 UAT hours logged here because the R2 ticket hadn't been created and ALMA UAT reporting wasn't aligned with the team.
✅
R3 – Completed
R3 completed April 2026 with a +59% overall saving — the best performing release. All areas delivered savings, with Production (+95%) and Testing (+75%) leading the results.
📊
Forward Simulation Assumptions
R4–R6 projections are based on observed velocity trends with diminishing returns. Assumes no new technical debt, stable team composition, and management budgets of 150–200h per release.
